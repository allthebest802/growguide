National Flower of Uzbekistan

Tulip — Tulipa — the flower that came from Central Asia

The national flower of Uzbekistan is the tulip — known locally as "Lola." This is botanically significant: despite the tulip's strong association with the Netherlands, the flower is native to Central Asia, and Uzbekistan is home to some of the most extraordinary wild tulip species on earth. The tulip made its way from Central Asia to Turkey, and from Ottoman Turkey to Europe in the sixteenth century — beginning the centuries-long obsession that would eventually produce "Tulipomania" in the Netherlands.

🇬🇧 UK Growing Tip Plant tulip bulbs in November in well-drained soil in full sun. Species tulips (greigii, kaufmanniana) perennialise reliably. Standard garden tulips: lift after flowering or treat as annuals.

Where do tulips really come from?

Wild tulips are native to a band of mountainous terrain stretching from Turkey and the Caucasus through Central Asia — including modern Uzbekistan, Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan — to western China. The Tien Shan mountains of Central Asia are a particular centre of wild tulip diversity. Uzbekistan alone is home to more than twenty wild tulip species, some of them found nowhere else in the world. These wild species — with their typically smaller, more delicate flowers in vivid red, yellow, orange and white — look quite different from the large, round-petalled garden tulips familiar from Dutch cultivation.

Uzbekistan's wild tulip heritage

In Uzbekistan, wild tulips carpeting the steppe and foothills in spring are a celebrated seasonal event. Species like Tulipa greigii, Tulipa kaufmanniana and Tulipa fosteriana are native to the region and were among the earliest species introduced to European gardens — they remain popular today. The Zaamin National Park and the foothills of the Tien Shan are particularly celebrated for wild tulip displays in April. Conservation of these wild species is increasingly important as agricultural expansion and collection pressure threaten native populations.

Growing tulips in UK gardens

Garden tulips are among the most rewarding and reliable spring bulbs for UK gardeners. Plant bulbs in November — later than most spring bulbs — in well-drained soil in full sun. Depth should be approximately three times the bulb's height. Tulips perform best in free-draining soil; in heavier soils, add grit to the planting hole or grow in raised beds. For species tulips (closer to the wild Uzbek originals) — Tulipa greigii, kaufmanniana and fosteriana — plant in a sunny, well-drained spot where they will reliably perennialise and return each year. Lift and dry standard garden tulips after flowering if you want to reuse the bulbs, or treat them as annual bedding for the most reliable display.

Flor nacional de Uzbekistán

La flor nacional de Uzbekistán es el tulipán (Tulipa), conocido localmente como "Lola". Aunque el tulipán se asocia con los Países Bajos, es originario de Asia Central — Uzbekistán alberga más de veinte especies de tulipanes silvestres nativos. La imagen de tulipanes silvestres cubriendo las estepas y colinas en primavera es uno de los espectáculos naturales más celebrados del país.
