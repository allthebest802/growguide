National Flower of Senegal

Baobab Flower — Adansonia digitata

The national flower of Senegal is the Baobab flower — the bloom of the baobab tree, Adansonia digitata, known across West Africa as the Tree of Life. One of the world's most distinctive and long-lived trees, the baobab can survive for over a thousand years and produces large, waxy white flowers that open at night and are pollinated by bats.

🇬🇧 UK Growing Tip Grow from seed as a container plant. Use very free-draining sandy compost. Full sun essential. Water sparingly in winter — rot is the main risk. Keep frost-free year-round.

What is the baobab tree and why is it special?

The baobab (Adansonia digitata) is unmistakable — a massive, swollen trunk that can store thousands of litres of water, topped by a relatively small canopy of branches that, when leafless in the dry season, look like roots reaching into the sky. This upside-down appearance has given rise to the legend that the baobab was planted upside-down by God as punishment for its pride. The tree lives for extraordinary periods — some specimens are believed to be over 2,000 years old. In Senegal and across West Africa, the baobab is central to daily life: its fruit, leaves, bark and seeds all provide food, medicine and materials.

What does the baobab flower look like?

Baobab flowers are large — up to 12cm across — with five broad white petals surrounding a dense mass of white stamens. They hang pendulously from the branches on long stalks and open only at night, when they emit a faint, slightly fermented scent that attracts fruit bats, bush babies and hawkmoths as pollinators. By morning the flowers have fallen, covering the ground beneath the tree in white. Despite the tree's enormous stature, the flowers have a delicate, almost magnolia-like appearance.

Growing baobab in the UK

Baobab trees cannot survive UK winters outdoors — they need frost-free conditions year-round. However, young baobabs can be grown from seed as fascinating specimen container plants. Seeds germinate readily after soaking for 24 hours in warm water. Grow in very free-draining, sandy compost with as much sun as possible. The seedlings grow slowly and develop their characteristic swollen base over several years. In winter, water very sparingly — the tree is dormant and rot is the main risk. Baobab powder, made from the dried fruit pulp, is widely available in UK health food shops as a nutritional supplement.

Flor nacional de Senegal

La flor nacional de Senegal es la flor del Baobab (Adansonia digitata), el árbol sagrado de África Occidental conocido como el Árbol de la Vida. Sus flores son grandes y blancas, se abren por la noche y son polinizadas por murciélagos. El baobab puede vivir más de mil años y es central en la cultura, alimentación y medicina senegalesa. Es uno de los árboles más icónicos del mundo.
