National Flower of Qatar

Sidr Tree — Ziziphus spina-christi

The national flower of Qatar is associated with the Sidr tree — Ziziphus spina-christi — an ancient, drought-tolerant tree of enormous cultural, religious and ecological significance across the Arabian Peninsula and North Africa. The Sidr tree is mentioned in the Quran and has been cultivated in the Middle East for thousands of years, prized for its fruit, its honey and its medicinal properties.

🇬🇧 UK Growing Tip Try the hardier Ziziphus jujuba (Chinese jujube) as a UK alternative — against a south-facing wall in mild areas. Sidr honey is available from Middle Eastern food suppliers in the UK.

What is the Sidr tree?

Ziziphus spina-christi is an evergreen or semi-evergreen tree native to arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East, including Qatar, Saudi Arabia, Egypt and Sudan. It can grow to 10 metres and is remarkably drought-tolerant, surviving on minimal rainfall in desert and semi-desert conditions. The tree produces small, cream to yellow flowers in clusters, followed by small round fruits (similar to jujubes or Chinese dates) that are eaten fresh, dried or made into juice. The flowers yield an exceptionally prized honey — Sidr honey from Yemen is among the most expensive honeys in the world.

Cultural and religious significance of the Sidr tree

The Sidr tree is mentioned in the Quran (as Sidrat al-Muntaha — the Lote Tree of the uttermost boundary) and has deep significance in Islamic tradition. Sidr leaves are used in the ritual washing of the deceased. The tree has been used in traditional medicine across the Arab world for millennia — its leaves, bark, fruit and roots all have documented medicinal applications. In Qatar, the Sidr tree represents endurance, continuity and deep roots in the desert landscape that has shaped the nation's identity.

Can Sidr tree be grown in the UK?

Ziziphus spina-christi requires hot summers, low humidity and good drainage — conditions that are difficult to replicate in the UK. It is occasionally grown in very sheltered, sunny spots in the warmest parts of England, or as a container plant overwintered in a frost-free greenhouse. The related Ziziphus jujuba (Chinese jujube) is hardier and more commonly available in UK specialist nurseries — it can be grown against a south-facing wall in milder UK regions and produces edible fruit. For Sidr honey, it is available in specialist UK delicatessens and online from Middle Eastern food suppliers.

Flor nacional de Qatar

La flor nacional de Catar se asocia con el árbol Sidr (Ziziphus spina-christi), un árbol de gran importancia cultural, religiosa y ecológica en la Península Arábiga.
