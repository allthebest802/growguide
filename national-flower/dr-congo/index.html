National Flower of DR Congo

Congolese Orchid — extraordinary forest biodiversity

The national flower of the Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC) is the Congolese Orchid — reflecting the country's extraordinary botanical heritage. The DRC contains one of the world's largest tropical rainforests and is home to thousands of plant species found nowhere else on earth, including hundreds of native orchid species from the Angraecum, Bulbophyllum and related genera.

🇬🇧 UK Growing Tip Angraecum orchids need 15°C+ year-round, high humidity and bright indirect light. Grow in bark compost. Water when nearly dry. Specialist plants for heated greenhouses.

The DRC's extraordinary orchid diversity

The Congo Basin rainforest is one of the world's most biodiverse ecosystems, second only to the Amazon in size. Within it, orchids are extraordinarily diverse — epiphytic species grow on the bark and branches of forest trees, drawing moisture from humid air and nutrients from decomposing organic matter. Angraecum species, known for their star-shaped white flowers and often powerful night-time fragrance, are among the most celebrated. Bulbophyllum species, the most species-rich orchid genus, includes hundreds of DRC representatives with extraordinary floral forms.

What is the national flower of the Democratic Republic of Congo?

The DRC does not have a single universally agreed official national flower in the way that some nations do, but the orchid — particularly African tropical orchid species native to the Congo Basin — is the most widely cited national floral symbol. The choice reflects the nation's identity as guardian of one of the world's greatest concentrations of plant life and biodiversity. The DRC's forests are under increasing pressure from deforestation, making this botanical identity all the more significant.

Growing African orchids in the UK

African tropical orchids from the Congo Basin require a heated greenhouse or heated indoor environment to thrive in the UK. Angraecum species need temperatures above 15°C year-round, high humidity, bright indirect light and careful watering — they should dry slightly between waterings and be grown in very free-draining bark-based compost. Many Angraecum species produce powerfully fragrant white flowers, particularly at night. They are specialist plants for experienced orchid growers, but richly rewarding when successfully established.

Flor nacional del Congo

La flor nacional de la República Democrática del Congo se asocia con las orquídeas del bosque tropical congoleño — particularmente las especies del género Angraecum y afines. El Congo alberga uno de los mayores bosques tropicales del mundo, con una diversidad de orquídeas extraordinaria. La orquídea congoleña simboliza la riqueza botánica única de este país africano.
